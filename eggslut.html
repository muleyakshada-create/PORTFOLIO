Eggslut

Multiple Locations · Singapore · Shoreditch, London · Retail Brand Rollout

"Translating a Los Angeles identity into scalable design strategies for diverse international markets — where spatial consistency, operational efficiency, and the open kitchen concept travel across cultures."

Eggslut began as a cult LA food brand defined by an open kitchen, a tight material palette, and an unapologetically casual identity. Taking that identity international meant designing for markets as different as Singapore and London — each with their own spatial constraints, building types, and customer expectations.

I contributed to the retail brand development across international locations, leading material palette exploration, renderings, and test fits to support spatial consistency and operational efficiency. The challenge was not replication but translation — maintaining brand legibility while responding to each site's character.

Design Approach

Brand rollout is not replication — it is translation. Each location carries the Eggslut DNA while responding to its own city, site, and customer.

The open kitchen is the heart of every Eggslut location — the design always departs from that central element and works outward. Material warmth, casual counter seating, and an unapologetic rawness in the finishes are consistent threads. What changes is how those elements respond to each site's spatial language.

Singapore demanded a tropical lightness and a precise fit within a tight commercial footprint. Shoreditch invited a grittier, more industrial interpretation — Eggslut's LA informality mapped onto East London's warehouse aesthetic.

Open kitchen as anchor — the defining spatial and experiential element that travels across all locations
Material palette exploration — warm, tactile, informal finishes calibrated to each market's sourcing and character
Operational efficiency — floor plan and test fit logic supporting high-volume service in compact footprints
Brand palette concept — a defined color, material, and finish system ensuring visual consistency across international rollout
Axonometric communication — spatial logic and brand identity expressed in plan and three-quarter view for client and contractor clarity
Exterior and interior continuity — the brand experience begins at the street, not the threshold
