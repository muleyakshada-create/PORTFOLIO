Gensler Research Institute · Equitable Neighborhood Start Up Fund

Finance the Future:
Strategies for Equitable
Neighborhood Development

This research challenges the assumption that equitable development requires subsidies or sacrificed returns. Through stakeholder interviews and case study analysis, we demonstrate that mission-driven neighborhood development can be revenue-generating, scalable, and financially competitive with market-rate development.

The research maps real-world models across six developer typologies — from large corporate developers to community co-ops — identifying the specific mechanisms that make equitable development work financially.

02 — Setting the Stage
The Problem
While hesitation holds many developers back, the tide has shifted. We must develop in a socially responsible way — and we now know how.
The challenge

There is significant hesitation among developers toward equitable and affordable housing — rooted in assumptions about profitability and risk that this research challenges directly.

The question

We know equitable neighborhood development is important and in high demand — but what are the real mechanisms for funding and driving successful development models?

The approach

How can we find real-time success stories and lessons learned on equitable neighborhood business models — and package them into a call to action for future developers?
