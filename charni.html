Design Studio · Sir JJ College of Architecture · 2017

Charni Road → Khotachiwadi

Urban Interventions · Mumbai, India · 500m Stretch

"Intervening in an urban site requires conscious sensitivity and appropriate justifications — the smallest alteration can result in a massive change."

Charni Road is one of Mumbai's prominent Western Line stations, surrounded by the historically rich Girgaon Chowpatty and Taraporewala Aquarium. At its end lies Khotachiwadi — a low-rise, high-density heritage settlement established by Mumbai's East Indian community, declared a heritage precinct.

The project draws people toward Khotachiwadi by creating an experiential journey along the 500m stretch between station and settlement — reinterpreting the architectural language of the wadi through modern street-level interventions that blend with the surrounding urban fabric rather than compete with it.

Design Approach

A journey designed using Ian Gehl's principles — mapping activity, identifying spatial character, then proposing interventions that respond to how people already use the street.

The project began with intensive site mapping: traffic patterns, street furniture, ground floor usage, and time-bound activities along the full 500m stretch. Spatial road sections were identified and analyzed before any design proposal was developed.

The design strategy was to interpret and reapply Khotachiwadi's architectural language — its Indo-Portuguese elements, compound walls, and scale — through modern street furniture, canopies, signage, and public space design. The wadi becomes the design vocabulary for the journey toward it.

6 Interventions
Intervention 01
Hinduja College Lane
Formalizing the hawker cluster into a shared urban plaza with segregated vending units, flexible multi-use space, and facilities for both hawkers and pedestrians.
Intervention 02
Daryush Bakery Junction
A common street roof and redesigned sidewalks at the bakery junction — signage regulation and canopy design to shelter pedestrians and formalize informal activity.
Intervention 03
SBI Building Plaza
A sheltered plaza in the setback of the SBI building — covered in Indo-Portuguese style columns, creating the most robust and versatile public space on the site.
Intervention 04
Shop Frontages
Guidelines regulating signage and projecting shopfronts to create a consistent, pedestrian-protective canopy along the ground floor commercial strip.
Intervention 05
Pedestrianization
Raja Ram Mohan Roy Road redesigned for barrier-free pedestrian movement — shared parking proposal between residential and commercial buildings to free road space.
Intervention 06
Khotachiwadi Entrance
The wadi's compound wall activated as a cultural canvas — murals, artwork, and media facades creating an identity and landmark arrival experience at the entrance.

Intervention 01
Hinduja College Lane
The junction of Raja Ram Mohan Roy Road and Hinduja College Lane is heavily crowded by street food hawkers. A dedicated, segregated space for these hawkers — combined with a multi-use plaza for pedestrians and a full set of street facilities — ensures the space remains clean, congestion-free, and activates for festivals and events.

Intervention 02
Daryush Bakery Junction
A common street roof and redesigned sidewalks at the bakery junction — facilitating pedestrians and on-street parking while regulating the signage of the commercial ground floor. The street roof draws on Khotachiwadi's Indo-Portuguese architectural vocabulary, creating a covered informal activity zone.

Intervention 03
SBI Building Plaza
The SBI building is set back from the main road — making its forecourt the most robust and versatile public space on the site. A new covered roof in Indo-Portuguese style, with a colonnade of designed support columns, transforms the setback into a sheltered plaza that attracts people to stop, sit, and become part of the area.
