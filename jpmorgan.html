JPMorgan Financial Center

Napa, California · Adaptive Reuse · Retail Banking

"JPMorgan's latest Financial Centers are redefining the in-person banking experience — reimagined as hospitality-driven environments for affluent clients through the strategic conversion of existing First Republic Bank locations."

The Napa location exemplifies this transformation: an existing financial center converted into a warm, contemporary environment with a carefully curated palette of wood finishes, soft lighting, and lounge settings that create a welcoming, highly personalized atmosphere aligned with JPMorgan's evolving retail banking standards.

I contributed to this adaptive reuse project across schematic design, construction documentation, and permit drawing development — and supported construction administration to ensure design intent was maintained through execution, coordinating with consultants and stakeholders throughout delivery.

Design Approach

Converting a bank branch into a hospitality-driven client experience — where every material and spatial decision reinforces a sense of welcome, warmth, and personalized service.

The conversion strategy focused on replacing the transactional character of a traditional bank branch with the warmth and intentionality of a private lounge. The program is organized around distinct zones — reception, social areas, transaction spaces, private offices, and a coffee bar — each designed to serve a different moment in the client journey.

Material choices were central to the transformation: wood finishes, soft lighting, and curated furnishings work together to signal departure from the institutional and arrival in something more personal — consistent with JPMorgan's evolving premium retail banking identity.

Warm material palette — wood finishes, soft lighting, and curated lounge furniture replacing institutional bank aesthetics
Hospitality zoning — reception, social area, transaction zone, private offices, and coffee bar each given distinct spatial character
Adaptive reuse — working within the existing First Republic building shell while achieving a full transformation of the client experience
Brand alignment — design decisions calibrated to JPMorgan's evolving retail banking standards and affluent client expectations
Coffee bar integration — a hospitality amenity that reframes the waiting experience and reinforces the lounge atmosphere
Private office design — individual client meeting rooms with refined finishes for high-net-worth advisory conversations
