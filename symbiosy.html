Symbiosy

A supermarket of crowd-sourced labor

"The 'flattened' nature of the internet compresses the notion of journey into a split-second click. How can we rethink the navigation of information by reorganizing it spatially?"

Symbiosy is a speculative metaverse — a unique supermarket, a collective of human services, an enormous bank of labor opportunities in a single wholesome world. It asks what happens when we apply the spatial logic of a supermarket to the crowd-sourced economy of the internet.

The project proposes a world of thousands of people — novice skill, intelligence, and talent at every nook and corner — made by us, for us, where every person is of equal importance owing to the work they do.

The Concept

What if the gig economy had a physical form — a supermarket you could walk through, aisle by aisle, choosing the labor you need?

The supermarket and crowd-sourcing share one thing in common: the Compartment. Compartmentalization as a unit — this is the structural logic of Symbiosy. In this metaverse, an unending number of micro-tasks and their micro-worlds (essentially workplaces) come together to build a macro world of multiple opportunities.

The market has aisles — endlessly long and ever rising in height, because it keeps adding new resources by the second. Every cell in an aisle looks different; it's hard to understand why two adjacent units are next to each other. Only when you arrive with a purpose will the world make sense, because the units and their arrangement fall into place based on the context you are looking for.

The Flattened Internet
Navigation today collapses journey into a click. Symbiosy restores the spatial in-between — the threshold, the aisle, the act of wandering with purpose.
02
Compartmentalization
The unit cell — shared logic between the supermarket shelf and the gig economy task. Each micro-world is self-contained yet arranged within a larger system.
03
Day & Night Horizon
In this world there is day at one end of the horizon and night at the other — a spatial metaphor for the always-on, time-zone-agnostic nature of crowd-sourced labor.
04
Identity & Purpose
The world is defined by singular identities — workplaces of multiple natures — yet willing to collaborate. One customizes their purpose through the micro-identities of the workforce.
The Analogy
Supermarket

Departments — distinct zones by type
Checkout — transaction and exchange
Window shopping — browsing without purpose or context
The Compartment
Crowd-sourced Labor
Categories — types of micro-tasks organized spatially
Micro-world — individual worker, individual workplace
Skill domains — distinct zones by expertise
Contract — task accepted and completed
Browsing — exploring labor without a defined need
The World of Symbiosy
Structure
Aisles that are endlessly long and ever-rising in height — because the market keeps adding new resources by the second. Every cell looks different; arrangement only makes sense with context.
Atmosphere
Day at one end of the horizon, night at the other. The world never sleeps. Every form of labor is unique yet fits into its aisle — a play of singular identities within a collaborative whole.
Experience
Walk through every aisle, through every category. You choose what suits you. Only when you arrive with a purpose do the units and their arrangement fall into place — the market is legible only through intent.
