UCLA Summer Studio · 2019

Trans(re)form.surface

Surface · Solid · Morphology · AR / VR · Fabrication

"Exploring the different patterns on surfaces — iso-curve, grid, contours — and using the surface as an interactive element to analyze the relation between surface and solid geometries."

Starting with four famous sculptures by contemporary artists, each team member precisely remodeled their assigned piece. These models were then fragmented — disassembled according to the nature of each sculpture — to generate a matrix of parts. From that matrix, a kit-bashed object was built, which became the base of the morphological study.

Morphologies were created through strategies of proliferation, scale, rotation, and volume transformation. The chosen morphology was animated, rendered with multiple color strategies, physically fabricated, and extended into AR and VR experiences — then finally documented using robotic cameras.

The Concept

Surface as active participant — not a boundary between inside and outside, but a generator of form, pattern, and spatial logic.

The project investigates how different surface patterns — iso-curves, grids, contours — create distinct spatial behaviors when applied to geometry. By using the surface as an interactive analytical tool, the project probes the relationship between the membrane and the mass it defines.

The kit-bashing strategy was not arbitrary: it preserved the DNA of each original sculpture while generating something entirely new — a hybrid object whose parts are traceable to their sources but whose whole belongs to none of them.

01
Iso-Curve
Lines of equal value tracing the topology of a surface — revealing curvature and flow as spatial information.
02
Grid
A rational overlay on an irrational surface — the tension between regularity and organic form made visible.
03
Contour
Horizontal slices through a 3D body — abstracting three-dimensional form into stacked two-dimensional information.
04
Point Library
A localization strategy applied to the main surface — identifying curve movement and establishing spatial coordinates.
Morphology Strategies
Proliferation
Multiplying elements across the object's surface — density as a design variable.
Scale
Proportional transformations of the kit-bashed object — exploring how size changes spatial reading.
Rotation
Angular transformations that reveal new orientations and shadow relationships within the form.
Volume Form
Modifying the volumetric character — pushing between solid mass and open lattice.
