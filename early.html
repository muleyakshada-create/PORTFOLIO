Early Intervention Center

Aurangabad, India · Solo Project

"A trans-disciplinary center to provide the correct positive environment for early intervention services, special education, and stimulus — through visual perception of form and space — for children to optimize their development."

With an annual birth cohort of almost 27 million, India is expected to have the largest number of infants born with birth defects globally. Developmental impairment affects approximately 10% of the childhood population — yet specialized early intervention infrastructure remains critically under-resourced.

This project responds to that gap: a purpose-designed facility in Aurangabad targeting children with special needs under India's Rashtriya Bal Swasthya Karyakram (RBSK) healthcare scheme. The design foregrounds sensory experience, therapeutic space, and child-centered spatial logic as its primary design drivers.

Design Principles
01
Sensory Environment
Every space calibrated for sensory experience — light, shadow, texture, sound, and scale tuned to the developmental needs of the primary user: the child.
02
Courtyard Logic
Internal courtyards as the organizational spine — providing natural light, outdoor play, and sensory variety within a protected, legible campus structure.
03
Child-Scaled Detail
Windows, thresholds, and fixtures designed to the height and reach of children — the building reads and functions differently for its youngest users.
04
Trans-disciplinary Program
Therapy, education, and assessment co-located in a single facility — enabling multi-disciplinary care without the child moving between distant institutions.
05
Connecting Roof
A single roof plane that links the internal building blocks — creating spatial continuity, shared identity, and a unifying shelter across the campus.
06
Form & Space as Stimulus
Visual perception of form and space as an active therapeutic tool — the architecture itself is part of the intervention program.
