"The line between real and fiction which was once so crisp — now seems to be blurring out. WHERE IS IT TAKING US?"

As newcomers to LA, we became fascinated by the city's ambiguity — a place that embraces millennial screen culture while remaining deeply rooted in its street art traditions. These murals, so often used as mere backdrops for photographs, are worlds unto themselves.

With the hyper-real pressure to curate a perfect social image, we asked: what if these flat 2D street artworks could be re-imagined as full 3D spatial experiences? What if the fiction working its way into reality could be made visible — and navigable?

The Concept

LA's street art as a portal — 2D murals reimagined as 3D spatial worlds in a hyper-real, screen-obsessed city.

An anecdote of the LA story — an experience navigating the streets of the Arts District in a hyper-real world where the quest is to take the perfect picture, and these murals become something far greater than static backdrops.

The project explores how augmented reality and spatial visualization could transform the relationship between viewer, image, and city — turning each mural into a doorway into its own narrative world.

01
Hyper-Reality
The blurring line between physical and digital experience in contemporary urban life — and the obsession with the perfect image.
02
Street Art as World
Each mural in LA's Arts District contains its own universe. The project asks what happens when we step through, not just in front of it.
03
2D → 3D Space
Using motion tracking and projection mapping to transform flat painted surfaces into navigable, inhabitable three-dimensional environments.
04
The AR Interface
A speculative app that guides users to the perfect picture — predicting compositions, suggesting hashtags, and revealing hidden spatial dimensions of each mural.
Technical Approach
Process
01
Live footage capture — filming on location in LA's Arts District, documenting murals in their urban context
02
Motion tracking in Cinema 4D — tracking the live footage frame-by-frame to lock 3D geometry to the physical wall surface
03
Projection mapping — superimposing a single mural frame as a spatial projection, seamlessly blending the 2D artwork into a 3D environment
04
Spatial character extraction — the mural's own visual language becomes the geometry, texture, and spatial logic of the 3D world it generates

Process — Storyboard

The story sets out on the streets of Los Angeles — the mural capital of the world. These streets prove to be a great backdrop for navigating a hyper-real world where an augmented AR app guides the viewer toward the perfect picture, complete with future predictions and optimal hashtags.

Each flat 2D artwork is re-imagined as a 3D world — because each one encompasses its own story and spatial logic waiting to be inhabited.

The storyboard represents the preliminary draft — a sketch-based pre-visualization of the film's narrative arc, from street encounter to spatial immersion to digital exit.
